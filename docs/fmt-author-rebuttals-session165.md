## Part 1: On "Confirmed Predictions" Being Misleading

**The critique:** The five confirmed predictions aren't uniquely novel; other theories or prior work accommodate them.

**Author's response:** I didn't know these works when I developed FMT (2015). The predictions are still predictions that FMT generates from its axioms. While they don't uniquely diagnose FMT, they rank every theory that CANNOT accommodate these facts below FMT. The predictions validate FMT's consistency — the fact that others also predicted them doesn't weaken FMT, it weakens theories that can't match them.

**Action needed:** Add proper citations to prior/parallel work without weakening the prediction claim. Frame as: "FMT independently derived these expectations, subsequently confirmed, which is consistent with the theory but not unique to it. However, theories that cannot accommodate these findings are thereby challenged."

## Part 2: Responses to "FMT's Biggest Weaknesses"

### 1. Mathematical Formalization

**The critique:** No math blocks research programs and journals.

**Author's response:** It makes no sense to create a mathematical formalization of a model that has not yet seen peer review. It is unreasonable to expect both the model AND the mathematics from a single person who isn't a mathematician. If the model is clearly specified, mathematical formalization should be purely mechanical — done in seconds by a capable AI system. The formalization is a follow-on task, not a prerequisite for theoretical validity.

**Implication:** The paper should explicitly frame this: "Formalization is a natural next step once the conceptual architecture has been validated through peer review. The theory is specified at a level of precision that makes formalization tractable."

### 2. Neural Specificity

**The critique:** FMT doesn't specify which brain areas implement which models.

**Author's response (correcting a fundamental misunderstanding):**
- It IS established research where IWM and ISM are in the brain. That's not FMT's job to rediscover.
- But this is beside the point of a theory of consciousness. The implicit models are just as remote from consciousness as the nerve endings in a small toe.
- **Critical correction:** Criticality is NOT a neurotransmitter question. It is the EXPLICIT models that require criticality — and that criticality is just as VIRTUAL as the explicit models themselves. It MAY be sourced from physical-level criticality, but need not be.
- Only the implicit models require criticality for the computation of consciousness. While it is mathematically impossible to have criticality arise from a lower-than-Class-4 system, this is not the research area of consciousness science.
- The criticality on which implicit models operate is just as virtual as the models are.

**This is a major clarification:** The paper may not make this clear enough — the virtual/physical distinction applies to criticality itself, not just to qualia. Reviewers may be confusing physical-substrate criticality with the computational-level criticality that FMT requires.

### 3. Single-Author / No Institutional Backing

**Author's response:** Hopefully solvable through outreach targets (D'Angiulli, Mediano, Shriki, etc.).

### 4. Unfalsifiability

**The critique:** "Permeability changed" can accommodate almost any finding.

**Author's response:** "Unfalsifiable" is a strong term. Any simpler model with the same empirical grounding would falsify FMT. To expect more would be unrealistic for something unobservable from outside. **Challenge to critics: Show me one competitor theory that is not yet falsified but IS falsifiable.** (IIT arguably failed COGITATE. GNW partially failed COGITATE. HOT, AST, RPT have never been decisively tested either.)

**This is a strong point.** The field's falsifiability standard is applied selectively — the "pseudoscience" letter against IIT was signed by 124 researchers precisely because falsifiability is contested across the board. FMT is no worse than competitors on this dimension.

### 5. Structure of Experience

**The critique:** FMT doesn't explain why EWM has specific phenomenological structure.

**Author's response:**
- The "structure" of experience is something only introspection can bring to the real world — unreliably, individually, and currently unmeasurably.
- We must assume that the "modality" of qualia correlates strongly with real physics: physics → sensory organs → clustering → qualia kinds.
- But synesthesia shows this is a soft classification. There are no "pure" qualia kinds.
- Being correlated to real-world physics is actually just an opinion, not an established fact.
- Therefore, demanding a theory explain "why red looks red" is asking the wrong question — the question assumes a fixed phenomenological structure that may not exist as assumed.

**Implication:** This could be a section in the paper or a standalone argument: the "structure of experience" requirement as typically stated contains hidden assumptions about the stability and universality of phenomenal structure that are empirically questionable.

## Part 3: GNW Critique (Devastating)

Matthias's point-by-point dismantling of GNW's explanatory claims. The core argument: GNW correlates with consciousness but EXPLAINS nothing about it.

### On the "Global Workspace" (Long-Range Connections)

"This says NOTHING about consciousness. If remote areas of the brain were less well connected, it would simply be a less efficient substrate. No-brainer. Does the avian brain have this? The octopus? Even without long connections, a number of hops gets you everywhere in the brain within 3-6 neurons. I call BS on that."

**Translation:** Long-range connectivity is a substrate optimization, not a consciousness mechanism. Small-world networks achieve global communication through short hop chains. GNW treats a substrate property as a consciousness property — a category error.

### On "Ignition"

"Sure, not surprising, but does this merely correlate with conscious experience? Does it explain ANYTHING? I can also gain conscious experience of a stimulus if I just hope for it long enough to occur. Either it is there and will be amplified, or if not it will be confabulated."

**Translation:** Ignition is the neural correlate of something crossing a threshold. But thresholds exist in every information-processing system. The fact that stronger signals get processed more thoroughly is trivially true. And confabulation shows you can have conscious experience WITHOUT the predicted stimulus ignition.

### On "Consciousness = Broadcast"

"Are there NO informations that are broadcasted but stay outside of conscious experience? I think strong correlation, no direct causation and definitely no explanatory power."

**Translation:** The identity claim (consciousness IS broadcast) is unfalsifiable if broadcasts that aren't conscious are explained away. And the explanatory gap remains: WHY does broadcast produce experience?

### On "Serial" Consciousness

"Consciousness does NOT 'feel' serial unless you are asked about your experience and forced to linearize it via language."

**Translation:** The "serial consciousness" claim conflates consciousness with verbal report. The workspace's limited capacity explains reporting bottlenecks, not the structure of experience itself.

### On "Subliminal Processing Explained"

"Not explained, correlated. Not having a strong signal in the brain OBVIOUSLY makes it harder for that signal to reach consciousness, by design. Explains nothing."

**Translation:** GNW explains subliminal processing the way "the light switch makes light" explains photons. Mechanistically correct, explanatorily empty.

### On "Unified" Consciousness

"Consciousness feels unified to a degree for most of us. There IS no single shared state."

**Translation:** The "unity" claim overstates the phenomenological evidence. DID, split attention, peripheral awareness, dreams — consciousness is messier than a single broadcast state.

### On "Flexible Behavior Needs Consciousness"

"The brain of a fly can do this."

**Translation:** Flexible behavior doesn't require the GNW architecture. Insects achieve flexible sensorimotor coordination without a prefrontal workspace. GNW conflates a specific neural implementation with a functional requirement.

### On "Attention Gates Consciousness"

"Attention cannot gate conscious experience. Conscious attention is after the experience, actual attention is automatic and simply adjusts the filter, but doesn't keep me from experiencing PTSD no matter how much I try not to pay attention."

**Translation:** The attention-consciousness link in GNW gets the temporal order wrong. Attention modulates the filter (implicit processing), but conscious experience can override attentional gates (PTSD intrusions, unbidden memories, pain). GNW's attentional gateway is a post-hoc rationalization that fails for involuntary experience.

## Part 4: The Darwin Parallel

FMT's situation is analogous to pre-Origin Darwin: the theory exists but lacks:
1. The empirical mountain (Darwin's 20 years of barnacles, pigeons, finches)
2. The institutional allies (Lyell, Hooker)
3. The accessible popular presentation (Origin of Species for educated laypeople)

FMT's advantage: unification (like Darwin's — one principle explains biogeography, morphology, embryology, fossils). That's the card to play.

## Strategic Value of These Rebuttals

These rebuttals reveal common misunderstandings that reviewers WILL have:

1. **Criticality confusion**: Reviewers will think FMT requires physical-substrate criticality. It doesn't — the criticality is virtual. This needs to be clearer in the paper.
2. **Formalization expectation**: Reviewers expect math from a theory paper. The paper needs a stronger defense of why formalization follows peer review, not precedes it.
3. **Neural specificity demand**: Reviewers want brain areas. FMT needs to articulate why a consciousness theory is NOT a neuroanatomy paper — and cite established work on where the implicit models reside.
4. **Prediction uniqueness**: The paper needs to reframe "confirmed predictions" as "empirical consistency that eliminates weaker theories" rather than "unique foresight."
5. **GNW's perceived strength**: Many reviewers treat GNW's correlational success as explanatory success. FMT needs a sharper section on why correlation ≠ explanation in consciousness science.
