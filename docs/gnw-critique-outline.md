# Why Global Workspace Theory Explains Nothing About Consciousness

**Working title for a standalone commentary/article**
**Target venues:** JCS, BBS commentary (if Seth target article), Frontiers in Psychology, or Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences

---

## Thesis

Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNW) is the most empirically productive framework in consciousness science. It has generated more experiments, more replicated findings, and more clinical applications than any competitor. It is also, on close examination, a theory of information routing that explains nothing about consciousness itself. GNW's empirical success is correlational — it identifies the neural signatures that accompany conscious access, not the mechanism that produces phenomenal experience. This distinction matters because mistaking correlation for explanation has shaped the field's experimental priorities for two decades.

## Structure (3,000-4,000 words)

### 1. GNW's Achievement Is Real

- Acknowledge the empirical program: ignition threshold, P3b, fronto-parietal involvement
- Acknowledge clinical utility: PCI for disorders of consciousness
- Acknowledge COGITATE participation as scientific courage
- This is not an attack on the research program, but a philosophical critique of the explanatory claims

### 2. The Broadcasting Mechanism Explains Access, Not Experience

**The core argument:**
GNW identifies consciousness with global broadcast: a mental state becomes conscious when it enters the workspace and is broadcast to all connected cortical areas. But this is an account of *access* — of when information becomes available for report, memory, and flexible use — not of *experience*.

**The radio analogy:**
A radio broadcasts information globally. Broadcasting is a mechanism for distribution, not a mechanism for creating the content being distributed. GNW tells us how the brain distributes information; it does not tell us why distribution creates experience.

**The trivial prediction problem:**
GNW's core empirical prediction — that stronger, attended stimuli are more likely to become conscious — is trivially true. In any information-processing system, signals above a threshold are processed more thoroughly than signals below it. Calling this "explaining consciousness" is like saying "the light switch explains photons." The mechanism is real; the explanation is empty.

### 3. The Correlation ≠ Explanation Gap

- GNW's findings (P3b, ignition, fronto-parietal activation) correlate with consciousness
- But correlation cannot be the endpoint for a field trying to explain the hardest problem in science
- The field's acceptance of GNW's correlational success as explanatory success has shaped experimental priorities: most consciousness experiments test whether a neural signature correlates with conscious report, rather than testing WHY any neural process produces experience
- This has been productive for cognitive neuroscience but has not advanced the science of consciousness itself

### 4. Four Counterexamples to GNW's Explanatory Claims

**4.1 "Why some things are conscious" → ignition threshold**
Rebuttal: Not having a strong signal obviously makes it harder for that signal to reach consciousness. This is a description of signal processing, not an explanation of consciousness. A hearing aid amplifies signals above threshold — does the amplification explain hearing?

**4.2 "Why consciousness feels unified" → broadcasting creates single shared state**
Rebuttal: Consciousness does NOT feel unified except when one is forced to linearize it through language. Split attention, peripheral awareness, background affect, proprioception — experience is multi-layered and partially contradictory. There is no single shared state. DID, split-brain cases, and normal phenomenology all show that "unity" is an oversimplification imposed by post-hoc verbal report.

**4.3 "Why consciousness is needed for flexible behavior" → global access**
Rebuttal: Insect brains achieve flexible sensorimotor coordination (foraging strategy, predator avoidance, social behavior in eusocial species) without a prefrontal workspace. If GNW's architecture is required for flexible behavior, flies should be automatons. They are not. The fly manages its sensory input, makes decisions, integrates memory — all without fronto-parietal broadcasting. GNW conflates a specific mammalian neural implementation with a functional requirement.

**4.4 "Attention is the gate to consciousness" → attentional control**
Rebuttal: If attention gates consciousness, involuntary conscious experience should be impossible. PTSD intrusions demonstrate otherwise: vivid, unwanted, fully conscious experience that overrides deliberate attentional control. Pain cannot be gated by attention. Dreams occur without attentional deployment. The attentional gateway model gets the temporal order wrong: conscious attention follows experience; it does not cause it. Actual attention (the implicit filter) is automatic and adjusts pre-consciously; conscious attention is a post-hoc narrative about where the filter pointed.

### 5. The Substrate Specificity Problem

- GNW requires specifically mammalian fronto-parietal infrastructure
- Corvids (nuclear pallium, no cortical columns) exhibit complex cognition and likely consciousness
- Octopuses (distributed nervous system, no central workspace) exhibit learning, problem-solving, possibly subjective experience
- Small-world network properties ensure any cortical region is reachable within 3-6 synaptic hops regardless of long-range connectivity
- GNW's "global workspace" may be a substrate optimization (faster routing), not a consciousness mechanism

### 6. What Would Actual Explanation Look Like?

- An actual explanation would tell us WHY broadcasting produces experience — not just that it correlates
- The real/virtual distinction (Four-Model Theory) offers one such account: broadcasting accelerates information integration within the virtual simulation, but the simulation — not the broadcast — is where experience resides
- Other accounts are possible (IIT's intrinsic causal power, PP's prediction error minimization)
- The point is not which explanation is correct, but that GNW doesn't even attempt one

### 7. The Damage of Confusing Correlation with Explanation

- Two decades of consciousness experiments have been shaped by GNW's framing
- The field has gotten excellent at measuring WHEN things become conscious (ignition, P3b, fronto-parietal activation)
- The field has made almost no progress on WHY any neural process produces experience
- This is partly because GNW's correlational success created the illusion that the explanatory work was done
- COGITATE's partial failure may be a healthy correction, but the field's experimental infrastructure is now optimized for access-consciousness research, not for Hard Problem research

### 8. Conclusion

GNW is the best theory of cognitive access and information routing in the brain. It is not a theory of consciousness. Calling it one has been productive for cognitive neuroscience but has diverted consciousness science from its central question. The field needs theories that explain WHY — not just WHEN and HOW — neural processing produces experience. GNW's empirical tools remain invaluable; its explanatory claims should be retired.
