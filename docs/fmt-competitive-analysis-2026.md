# FMT Competitive Analysis — March 2026

Research conducted Session 165 via three parallel agents (research-analyst, competitive-analyst, search-specialist). Brutally honest self-assessment of FMT's position in the consciousness theory landscape.

## Field Landscape

- **29+ distinct theories** (2021 scoping review); Frontiers 2025 says 200+ "approaches"
- Only 3-4 theories have significant research programs
- **COGITATE** (Nature, May 2025): adversarial collaboration tested IIT vs GNW; both damaged
  - IIT's central prediction (sustained posterior synchrony) **failed**
  - GNW's prefrontal ignition prediction **partially falsified** (encodes category but not identity/orientation; no offset ignition)
  - Data favored posterior/sensory cortex involvement → RPT-compatible
  - Koch publicly broke with IIT ("bet on the wrong horse")
  - Tononi defended by arguing COGITATE tested wrong operationalization (widely criticized as goalpost-moving)
- **IIT pseudoscience letter** (Sep 2023): 124 researchers signed; community survey: only 8% fully agree, 20% fully disagree, 60% uncommitted
- **ConTraSt database**: 85% of consciousness experiments are theory-supporting, not theory-testing (structural bias toward confirmation)

## Theory Rankings (Prospective Predictions Confirmed by Independent Labs)

1. **GNW** — best overall: ignition threshold, P3b, fronto-parietal involvement. 20 years of productive experiments. Challenged by COGITATE on specifics.
2. **RPT (Lamme)** — highest hit rate: TMS timing windows, VAN marker. Small program, clean results.
3. **PP/Seth** — strong on perceptual phenomenology: interoception, psychedelic precision-weighting. Weak on threshold question ("why conscious at all?").
4. **Metzinger (PSM)** — underappreciated; genuine prospective predictions on self-model disruption confirmed.
5. **HOT** — real findings but not distinctive from GNW empirically.
6. **IIT** — mathematically elaborate, empirically poor. COGITATE was its big test and it failed.
7. **AST** — conceptually inventive, limited distinctive empirical confirmation.

## FMT Genuine Strengths

1. **Virtual qualia / real-virtual split** — novel philosophical move. Dissolves Hard Problem without panpsychism or strong emergence. No other theory does this.
2. **Avoids Combination Problem** — real advantage over IIT.
3. **Unifies altered states** — psychedelics, anesthesia, anosognosia, DID, dreams under variable permeability. Competitors don't match this unification.
4. **Four novel predictions** — legitimately novel in framing. Prediction 1 (psychedelic-anosognosia) is strongest: cross-domain surprise no other theory generates.
5. **Meta-Problem** — ESM's structural opacity to own substrate explains why consciousness seems mysterious. Arguably better than AST's account.

## FMT "Five Confirmed Predictions" — Honest Assessment

**None are uniquely diagnostic for FMT. They validate criticality-as-mechanism, not FMT's four-model architecture specifically.**

| Claim | Honest Assessment | Also Predicted By |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Anesthetic-criticality convergence | Not novel by 2015. Carhart-Harris validated criticality-consciousness by 2012-2014. Casali et al. (2013) preceded FMT. | Any criticality-incorporating theory |
| Sleep-criticality restoration | Meisel et al. (2013) showed fading waking criticality before FMT | Theory-agnostic criticality finding |
| Sleep onset as bifurcation | General dynamical systems prediction | Multiple theories predict bifurcation transitions |
| Psychedelic hierarchy | REBUS (Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019) generates functionally identical prediction | PP/REBUS, visual neuroscience |
| Split-brain holographic degradation | Known since Gazzaniga. Only holographic mechanism is novel (unproven) | Any distributed consciousness model |

**Risk**: Reviewers who check references will see the predictions weren't uniquely novel. This is the biggest credibility risk in the paper's current framing.

## FMT Biggest Weaknesses

1. **No mathematical formalization** — IIT has Φ, PP has free energy. Blocks research programs and high-impact journals.
2. **No neural specificity** — which brain areas implement which models? GNW, IIT, PP all specify; FMT doesn't.
3. **Single-author / no institutional backing** — IIT has 50+ researchers, PP hundreds.
4. **Unfalsifiability risk** — "permeability changed" can accommodate almost any altered-state finding.
5. **Structure of experience underspecified** — *why* does EWM have specific phenomenological structure? IIT's qualia space addresses this; FMT doesn't.
6. **"Addresses all 8 requirements" claim is overrated** — honest audit: credible on ~5/8, reframes 2/8, underspecified on 1/8. Commensurate with competitors, not superior.

## Desk Rejection Analysis

- **arXiv, bioRxiv, PsyArXiv, PhilSci-Archive** = scope mismatches, not quality signals
- **NoC ×2** = substantive: non-standard definition + "predictions too general" + factual REM error (now fixed)
- **Zero peer reviews ever** = the theory has never been evaluated by domain experts; all rejections were editorial

## Likely Outcome

**"Metzinger-class" theory**: intellectually influential, cited in reviews, discussed at conferences, never the consensus paradigm. Competitive analyst rated FMT 6/10 — real merit, structural barriers.

## What FMT Needs to Become Truly Competitive

1. Mathematical formalization (define virtual qualia formally, derive predictions quantitatively)
2. Neural specificity (which areas implement which models)
3. Research ecosystem (co-investigators, graduate students)
4. Experimental victories (win at least one novel prediction decisively)
5. AI validation (proof-of-concept four-model system)

## Strategic Implications for Submissions

- Soften "five confirmed predictions" framing → "converging evidence" (which the paper already uses in §8.1 body text, but abstract oversells)
- The four novel predictions ARE the paper's distinctive contribution — lead with those
- PLREV/NBSR are better targets than NoC (scope match for theoretical reviews)
- Getting past an editor to actual peer review = the critical milestone
