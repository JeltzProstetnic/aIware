### Page 262 - Real/Virtual Split (CRITICAL DIAGRAM)
**Render**: `book_page_262_render.png`
**Images**: 8 images

**Main Diagram** (`book_page_262_img_1.png`, 291x198):
- **THE CORE INSIGHT**: "Virtuell, simuliert, explizit" vs "Real, erlernt, implizit"
- Left side: Weltmodell (World Model) containing Ich-Modell (I-Model) - VIRTUAL/SIMULATED/EXPLICIT
- Right side: Metamodell containing Selbstmodell (Self-Model) - REAL/LEARNED/IMPLICIT
- Arrow labeled "Simulation" from Metamodell to Weltmodell
- Arrow from "Reale Welt" (Real World) to Metamodell labeled "Lernvorgang" (Learning process)
- **THIS IS THE QUALIA DIAGRAM** - explains phenomenal consciousness as virtual simulation
- German text below explains qualia: colors don't exist in real world, only light frequencies detected by three cone types in retina. We construct color from learned color names. We even experience blue-lit tomatoes as red despite no red being detected. "Wir nehmen also nicht die echte Welt wahr, sondern ein Modell der Welt..."

### Page 264 - Phenomenological Content Timeline
**Render**: `book_page_264_render.png`
**Images**: 16 images (mostly text labels)

**Main Diagram**: Timeline showing "Phänomenologischer Gehalt" (phenomenological content) varying over time
- X-axis: time progression through daily events (waking up, brushing teeth, driving to work, arriving at work, etc.)
- Y-axis: intensity of phenomenological content
- Shows varying levels of conscious awareness throughout the day
- German examples: "Wecker geht ab; Oje, ich muss aufstehen!", "Zähneputzen", "Mit dem Auto in die Arbeit fahren", etc.
- Bottom bracket shows periods marked as "bewusst" (conscious)
- Demonstrates that consciousness is NOT constant but varies with self-model updates

German text below: The Metamodell (with Selbstmodell) is a real, relatively robust image of the world trained by the cortex. It has a physical substrate in neuronal connections. Explains stability and plasticity.
