## Detailed Journal Assessments

### 1. Physics of Life Reviews (Elsevier) -- TOP TARGET

**Impact Factor**: 14.3 (June 2025). CiteScore: ~30. Q1 across multiple categories.

**Scope**: Review articles on physics of living systems, complex phenomena in biological systems, artificial life, artificial intelligence. The journal explicitly aims to be "a unifying force going across barriers between disciplines, addressing all living systems from molecules to populations and from genetics to mind and artificial systems modeling these phenomena." This interdisciplinary, systems-theoretic scope is an excellent match for FMT.

**Why it fits FMT**:
- The journal has published consciousness-related review articles, including work on quantum approaches to consciousness and Orch OR theory
- The criticality/edge-of-chaos framework central to FMT aligns with the journal's physics-of-complex-systems identity
- The journal explicitly welcomes "artificial intelligent systems" -- FMT's substrate-independence and AC implications are relevant
- Long-form review/theory articles are the journal's core format

**Word limit**: No explicit word limit for published review articles. The proposal stage is 1,000-2,000 words; once accepted, the full review has no stated ceiling. Review articles in this journal routinely run 15,000-25,000+ words. FMT at ~16,744 words fits comfortably.

**Submission process**: Two-stage. First submit a Review Proposal (1,000-2,000 words) via Editorial Manager. If the editors find it suitable, you are invited to submit the full manuscript. This is both a barrier and an advantage -- if the proposal passes, desk rejection of the full paper is less likely. The journal also invites reviews from active researchers, but unsolicited proposals are accepted.

**Review time**: Remarkably fast -- median submission-to-first-decision of ~6 days for proposals. Full review cycle unclear but likely weeks.

**APC**: $4,510 (open access). As a hybrid journal, subscription publication has no APC.

**Independent researcher concern**: The journal evaluates work on merit. The proposal stage means editors assess the idea before seeing affiliations as the primary factor. No known bias against independent researchers, though the prestige level means competition is fierce.

**Risk assessment**: The two-stage process is actually helpful -- a proposal rejection wastes minimal effort. The main risk is that the editors may view FMT as insufficiently "physics" without empirical data. Framing the proposal around criticality, cellular automata, and physics of computation would help.

**Scope match**: 8/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 4/10 (high bar, but proposal stage helps manage risk)
**Prestige**: 9/10


### 2. Behavioral and Brain Sciences (Cambridge) -- ASPIRATIONAL

**Impact Factor**: 13.7. One of the most prestigious interdisciplinary journals in cognitive science.

**Scope**: Publishes "target articles" -- unusually significant theoretical articles that formally model or systematize a body of research -- accompanied by 20-40 open peer commentaries from specialists, plus author response. This format is almost perfectly designed for a comprehensive consciousness theory.

**Why it fits FMT**:
- BBS target articles are explicitly for "novel interpretation, synthesis, or critique of existing experimental or theoretical work" -- FMT is exactly this
- The open commentary format would generate enormous visibility and engagement
- The journal covers psychology, neuroscience, behavioral biology, and cognitive science -- FMT spans all of these
- Recent accepted manuscripts include work on consciousness and AI consciousness

**Word limit**: Target articles are substantial (typically 12,000-14,000 words for the main text). FMT at ~16,744 words may need modest trimming or could be negotiated.

**Submission process**: Three-stage. First a Target Article Proposal (1,000-2,000 words explaining why the article suits BBS format). If accepted, submit full article. If accepted for commentary, 20-30 commentators are invited. This is extremely selective.

**APC**: None (subscription journal). No cost to publish.

**Independent researcher concern**: BBS has published work from researchers without traditional affiliations, but the bar is very high. The proposal must demonstrate broad interdisciplinary significance and controversy potential. Having the Zenodo preprint with citations could help.

**Risk assessment**: Very low acceptance rate. However, the proposal costs almost nothing to try. If accepted, the open commentary format would be transformative for FMT's visibility. The requirement to suggest 20-30 commentators means you need to identify 20-30 researchers who would engage seriously with FMT.

**Scope match**: 9/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 3/10 (extremely selective, but the format is ideal)
**Prestige**: 10/10


### 3. Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews (Elsevier)

**Impact Factor**: 7.9 (June 2025). CiteScore: ~14. Q1 in neuroscience.

**Scope**: Review articles providing comprehensive, scholarly, in-depth coverage of a topic. Also publishes "theoretical articles" and "mini-reviews." The journal covers all neuroscience and biobehavioral topics including consciousness, criticality, and computational approaches.

**Why it fits FMT**:
- The journal publishes theoretical reviews of consciousness, including criticality-based frameworks
- Recent publications include work on self-organized criticality and consciousness (2024-2025)
- FMT's systematic comparison against 8 requirements and 7 competing theories reads like a comprehensive review
- The neurobehavioral angle (psychedelics, anesthesia, sleep, DID, split-brain) gives empirical hooks

**Word limit**: No stipulated word limit for review articles -- "reviews should be in depth, scholarly and comprehensive so that all relevant literature is included." Theoretical articles may have more restricted scope. FMT at ~16,744 words fits well as either a review or theoretical article.

**APC**: $4,740 (open access option). Subscription publication has no APC.

**Review time**: Generally fast by journal standards. Single-anonymized review.

**Independent researcher concern**: The journal focuses on the quality and comprehensiveness of the review. However, the emphasis on "review" means pure novel theory may face skepticism -- framing FMT as a comprehensive review that proposes a unifying framework would help.

**Risk assessment**: The key question is whether the editors classify FMT as a "review" (reviewing the consciousness landscape and proposing a synthesis) or as an "original theory paper" (which the journal may not prefer). The paper's structure -- surveying 8 requirements and 7 theories before presenting FMT -- naturally reads as a review with a theoretical proposal. The "theoretical article" category exists but may have different expectations.

**Scope match**: 7/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 4/10 (review-oriented journal may want more empirical grounding)
**Prestige**: 8/10


### 4. Consciousness and Cognition (Elsevier)

**Impact Factor**: 2.0 (June 2025). CiteScore: 4.2. Q1 quartile.

**Scope**: Natural-science approach to consciousness, voluntary control, and self. Features empirical research, theoretical articles, and "integrative theoretical and critical literature reviews."

**Why it fits FMT**:
- The journal explicitly accepts theoretical articles alongside empirical work
- "Integrative theoretical and critical literature reviews" describes FMT well
- The consciousness focus is exact -- no need to justify relevance
- The journal has published computational and systems-theoretic consciousness work

**Word limit**: Regular articles "typically occupy up to 14,000 words, but can be longer with submission of a cover letter justifying the need for additional length and permission of the Editor." FMT at ~16,744 words would need a cover letter explaining the length, but is within negotiable range. Review articles have no stated upper limit (minimum 4,000 words).

**APC**: Hybrid journal. Subscription publication is free. Open access option: $3,240.

**Review time**: Variable, typically 2-4 months.

**Independent researcher concern**: The journal appears welcoming to independent researchers. The editorial board is international and the scope statement doesn't mention affiliation requirements.

**Risk assessment**: This is the natural home for a consciousness theory paper. The main risk is that some editors lean toward empirical work and may view a purely theoretical paper skeptically. However, the explicit inclusion of "theoretical articles" in the scope mitigates this. The word count issue is manageable with a cover letter. The desk rejection risk is moderate -- the theory is novel and comprehensive, which could go either way with editors used to more incremental work.

**Scope match**: 9/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 6/10 (good scope match, but IF is lower = less selective = better odds)
**Prestige**: 6/10


### 5. Frontiers in Psychology: Consciousness Research

**Impact Factor**: 2.6 (parent journal). The Consciousness Research section is a specialty section.

**Scope**: "Explores our understanding of conscious experience as a natural phenomenon in humans and other animals, in health and in disease." The section has recently been renamed/merged to "Consciousness Research and Mindfulness."

**Why it fits FMT**:
- The "Hypothesis and Theory" article type is designed for papers like FMT
- Recent 2025 publications include consciousness theories (Extended Information Theory, consciousness measurement frameworks, evolution of consciousness)
- The section actively publishes novel theoretical frameworks
- Frontiers is very open to independent researchers

**Word limit**: "Hypothesis and Theory" articles have a maximum of 12,000 words. This is a PROBLEM -- FMT at ~16,744 words exceeds this by ~40%. Would require significant trimming, splitting into two papers, or negotiation with the editor.

**APC**: 3,150 CHF (~$3,500). No waiver for independent researchers in developed countries. This is a mandatory cost.

**Acceptance rate**: ~37% overall for Frontiers in Psychology (2024). The Consciousness Research section may differ.

**Review time**: ~77 days (median) to decision.

**Independent researcher concern**: Frontiers is very welcoming to independent researchers. No affiliation bias.

**Risk assessment**: The word limit is the major obstacle. If the paper can be trimmed to 12,000 words, this becomes one of the most reliable options. The open-access model means high visibility. The APC is significant but not extreme. The acceptance rate is reasonable. Frontiers' reputation, while improved, is still viewed skeptically by some traditional academics -- but the Consciousness Research section publishes serious work.

**Scope match**: 8/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 7/10 (open editorial stance, but word limit issue)
**Prestige**: 5/10


### 6. Journal of Consciousness Studies (Imprint Academic)

**Why it fits FMT**:
- Perfect scope match -- the journal exists for exactly this kind of paper
- The interdisciplinary, accessible language requirement matches FMT's style
- JCS publishes novel theories (not just empirical work)
- 98% of recent publications had "unrecognized affiliation" -- extremely friendly to independent researchers
- No APC (subscription model)
- The editorial philosophy explicitly asks authors not to assume specialist knowledge

**Word limit**: 9,000 words maximum (including abstract, footnotes, references). This is a DEALBREAKER for the full paper -- FMT at ~16,744 words is nearly double the limit. The trimmed version (9,019 words) was already at the JCS ceiling.

**Submission process**: Email submission (Word/RTF/PDF). Anonymized review. 150-word summary required.

**APC**: None.

**Independent researcher concern**: No issue. JCS is one of the most independent-researcher-friendly journals in the field.

**Risk assessment**: The word limit rules out the full paper. JCS could work for a significantly condensed version, but that's essentially the trimmed paper that was already desk-rejected by NoC. If you want to pursue JCS, it would need to be a different cut of the material or a focused argument rather than the comprehensive theory.

**Scope match**: 10/10
**Desk pass likelihood**: 7/10 (but only if within word limit)
**Prestige**: 5/10 (historically important, but low IF)

## TOP 3 RECOMMENDATIONS

### #1: Physics of Life Reviews (PRIMARY TARGET)

**Why**: Highest impact factor (14.3) that plausibly fits the paper. The journal's interdisciplinary, systems-theoretic scope aligns with FMT's approach. No word limit for reviews. The two-stage proposal process is risk-efficient -- a 1,500-word proposal costs almost nothing to try, and if it passes, you have editorial buy-in before writing the full submission.

**Strategy**:
1. Write a 1,500-word Review Proposal emphasizing: (a) the pre-paradigm state of consciousness science, (b) FMT as a physics-of-computation framework unifying 8 requirements no current theory addresses, (c) the independent convergence with empirical criticality literature, (d) the paper's systematic comparison across 7 competing theories
2. Frame FMT as a "review + synthesis" -- it reviews the consciousness landscape and proposes a unifying framework from a physics-of-living-systems perspective
3. Emphasize the criticality/edge-of-chaos framework and Wolfram computational classification -- this is the "physics" hook
4. If the proposal is rejected, you've lost only the proposal effort, not a full submission cycle

**Timeline**: Proposal decision in ~1 week. If accepted, full review submission and peer review in 2-3 months.

**Cost**: $4,510 if open access. Subscription option available (no cost).


### #2: Consciousness and Cognition (STRONG BACKUP)

**Why**: Perfect scope match for a consciousness theory paper. Explicitly publishes "integrative theoretical and critical literature reviews." The 14,000-word regular article limit can be extended with editorial permission. IF of 2.0 is lower but the journal is well-read in the consciousness research community. Hybrid model means no mandatory APC.

**Strategy**:
1. Submit as a "Review Article" (no upper word limit stated) rather than a regular article
2. Write a cover letter explaining the paper's comprehensive scope justifies the length -- it reviews 8 requirements and 7 theories before presenting FMT
3. Alternatively, negotiate the word count as a regular article with editorial permission
4. Emphasize the paper's systematic structure and testable predictions -- this is what C&C values
5. Submit in parallel with PLREV proposal if desired (most journals allow simultaneous submission to different journals only if using different manuscript types; check C&C policy -- Elsevier generally prohibits simultaneous submission)

**Timeline**: 2-4 months to first decision.

**Cost**: Free (subscription) or $3,240 (open access).


### #3: Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews (PRESTIGE BACKUP)

**Why**: High impact (7.9), no word limit, accepts theoretical articles alongside reviews. The paper's coverage of psychedelic phenomenology, anesthesia, sleep, split-brain, and DID gives strong neurobehavioral hooks. Recent publications on criticality and consciousness show editorial openness to this topic area.

**Strategy**:
1. Frame FMT as a "theoretical review" -- the paper systematically reviews the consciousness landscape and proposes a unifying neurobehavioral framework
2. Emphasize the neuroscience content: the six-layer cortex implementation, empirical predictions (psychedelic ego dissolution, anosognosia), clinical phenomena (DID, split-brain)
3. De-emphasize the more philosophical content (Hard Problem dissolution, meta-problem) in the cover letter while keeping it in the paper
4. Highlight the convergence with empirical criticality findings (Hengen & Shew 2025, Algom & Shriki 2026) -- this grounds the theory in current neuroscience

**Timeline**: Weeks to first decision (journal is relatively fast).

**Cost**: Free (subscription) or $4,740 (open access).
