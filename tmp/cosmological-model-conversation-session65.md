## Where This Could Break

### Weak Point 1: E = I is not proven
The energy-information identity is strongly suggested by Landauer, Bekenstein, and holographic principle, but not established as fundamental law. If they're merely correlated rather than identical, the conservation argument weakens.

### Weak Point 2: Class 4 as attractor is an empirical claim, not a theorem
Self-organized criticality is well-documented in many systems, but whether the *universe itself* self-organizes to Class 4 (rather than just containing Class 4 subsystems) is a much stronger claim.

### Weak Point 3: The singularity unification across scales is speculative
Whether Planck-scale structure, event horizons, and cosmological horizons are truly "the same thing" at different scales requires a theory of quantum gravity to verify. Loop quantum gravity and string theory both suggest something like this, but neither confirms it.

### Weak Point 4: Testability is limited
The model predicts: (a) singularities at all scales share informational properties (black hole information paradox resolution should confirm); (b) the universe's large-scale dynamics are at criticality (in principle testable through CMB analysis or large-scale structure statistics); (c) consciousness is the local instance of the pattern (testable through FMT predictions). But the core claim — that the universe IS an SB-HC4A — may be unfalsifiable from within, for exactly the Godelian reason the model itself predicts.

### Weak Point 5: The Cognitive Ceiling Problem (added by Gruber)
If we are Class 4 automatons operating at criticality, then the SB-HC4A model may simply be the most complex concept our Class 4 brains can produce. We cannot think in Class 5. We cannot conceive of structures beyond our own computational class. The pattern we see — Class 4 everywhere, self-similar at every scale, holographic and self-referential — might be the *signature of our own cognitive architecture projected onto the cosmos*, not a feature of the cosmos itself.

This is the ultimate epistemological limit: a Class 4 system can simulate anything *up to and including* Class 4 complexity, but it cannot verify whether the universe exceeds that. If the universe is Class 5 but locally appears Class 4 to Class 4 observers (because that's the maximum pattern we can detect), we would construct exactly this model — and be wrong.

This objection is structurally identical to the Meta-Problem at cosmic scale: the ESM cannot see its own substrate, so it cannot distinguish between "the universe has this structure" and "my brain can only model the universe with this structure." The cosmological model predicts its own potential unfalsifiability — which is either a devastating weakness or the deepest confirmation of the symmetry, depending on your philosophical disposition.
