## Detailed Analysis of Each Prediction

### Prediction 1: Distinct fMRI Signatures (2×2 Factorial)

**Existing literature**: The individual contrasts (self vs. world; implicit vs. explicit) are extremely well-studied. The default mode network (DMN) is established as the self-referential network. Implicit/explicit processing distinctions are a staple of cognitive neuroscience. What has NOT been tested is the specific 2×2 factorial interaction — i.e., whether crossing scope × mode yields a clean double dissociation in distributed networks.

**Problem**: While the factorial design is novel, the prediction is (a) not surprising given existing knowledge of DMN and sensory cortices, (b) vulnerable to "well, of course self-referential tasks activate DMN" criticism, and (c) confirmable doesn't mean distinguishing — most theories are compatible with functional specialization.

**Distinguishing power**: Low. IIT, GNW, PP, and HOT are all compatible with different tasks activating different networks. The 2×2 structure is a taxonomic claim, not a mechanistic one.

**Verdict**: MERGE with Prediction 9 (DID). The 2×2 factorial task battery is most useful as part of the DID experimental design — show that alter switches correspond to ESM-related network reconfigurations specifically.

### Prediction 2: Psychedelic Content Maps Cortical Hierarchy

**Existing literature**: Substantial.
- Klüver (1926): form constants (tunnels, spirals, lattices, cobwebs) at low doses
- Bressloff et al. (2002): mathematical model deriving form constants from V1 architecture
- Carhart-Harris et al. (2016): psilocybin increases V1 connectivity and DMN disintegration
- Timmermann et al. (2023): DMT dose-dependent effects on visual cortex
- Psilocybin V1-V3 dose effects (2025): 38% Ebbinghaus illusion increase at 5mg, 59% at 10mg
- Carhart-Harris & Friston (2019): REBUS model — psychedelics relax high-level priors, allowing bottom-up signals through

**Problem**: The REBUS model (predictive processing) already predicts hierarchical content emergence under psychedelics as reduced top-down constraint allows progressively deeper processing layers to "show through." This is essentially the same prediction as FMT's "increasing permeability exposes intermediate processing stages."

**Distinguishing power**: Very low. PP/REBUS generates a nearly identical prediction from different axioms.

**Verdict**: DROP. This prediction actively HURTS FMT because a reviewer familiar with REBUS will say "this is just the anarchic brain hypothesis restated."

### Prediction 3: Ego Dissolution Content Is Controllable

**Existing literature**: The "set and setting" literature acknowledges that environment matters for psychedelic experience, but this is always discussed in terms of emotional tone, anxiety levels, or general quality of experience. NO existing study has systematically manipulated sensory input during ego dissolution and measured whether the IDENTITY CONTENT (what the subject "becomes") tracks the dominant input.

Key evidence:
- Salvia divinorum produces extreme identity transformations — subjects report "becoming" objects, entities, or spatial configurations. This is consistent with FMT's prediction that a deprived ESM latches onto available input.
- Salvinorin A specifically: intense dissociative effects, blocking external sensory perception and modulating interoception and sense of body ownership (PMC4675976)
- Nour et al. (2016): Ego Dissolution Inventory — standardized measurement exists
- VR ego dissolution research: group VR can produce ego attenuation comparable to psychedelics (Nature, 2022) — this provides a controllable environmental paradigm

**Critical gap**: No study has ever asked: "During ego dissolution, if we control the dominant sensory input, does the identity content (who/what the subject becomes) track that input?" This is FMT's most distinctive prediction.

**Distinguishing power**: VERY HIGH.
- IIT: no mechanism for identity content determination during ego dissolution
- GNW: no mechanism
- HOT: no mechanism
- PP/REBUS: could generate a partial account (self-model relaxes, new priors form from input) but does NOT predict the specific input→identity tracking pattern. PP predicts the self-model dissolves but doesn't specify what replaces it.
- AST: no mechanism
- Metzinger SMT: closest competitor — dissolving PSM could allow environmental input to shape new self-model, but SMT doesn't generate the specific "latch onto dominant input" prediction

**Vulnerability to "too general"**: LOW — this is highly specific and no one else predicts it.

**Verdict**: KEEP. This is FMT's crown jewel.

### Prediction 4: Psychedelics Alleviate Anosognosia

**Existing literature**:
- Anosognosia mechanism: disconnection between implicit awareness (ISM equivalent) and explicit awareness (EWM/ESM equivalent). Patients show implicit awareness of deficits (behavioral indices) but no explicit awareness. Lesions to right angular/superior temporal gyrus, insula, basal ganglia.
- Psychedelics and stroke: emerging field. Algernon Health's AP-188 (DMT) Phase 2a for acute ischemic stroke (2025). Johns Hopkins PHATHOM study. Neuroprotective effects of psilocybin in rat stroke model (2024).
- Psychedelics and disorders of consciousness: Alnagger et al. (2026, Advanced Science) — virtual clinical trial of psychedelics for DoC patients. Scott & Carhart-Harris proposed psilocybin for increasing conscious awareness in DoC.
- NO study has ever tested psychedelics specifically for anosognosia.

**Critical insight**: The literature is CONVERGING toward this prediction from two directions:
1. Psychedelic stroke recovery research (neuroprotection, neuroplasticity)
2. Psychedelics for disorders of consciousness (complexity increase)
But nobody has connected the dots to anosognosia specifically. FMT provides the theoretical bridge: anosognosia = local permeability block; psychedelics = global permeability increase.

**Distinguishing power**: VERY HIGH.
- No other consciousness theory connects psychedelics and anosognosia through a single mechanism
- This is a genuine cross-domain surprise prediction
- If confirmed, it would be extremely strong evidence for the variable-permeability principle

**Vulnerability to "too general"**: LOW — extremely specific, testable, cross-domain.

**Practical concern**: Requires clinical trial with stroke patients. Ethically complex. BUT: sub-ego-dissolution doses are the claim, and psilocybin clinical trials already have extensive safety data. Could start with case reports or observational studies of psychedelic use by anosognosia patients.

**Verdict**: KEEP. Cross-domain surprise predictions are the gold standard for theory validation.

### Prediction 5: All Anesthetics Converge on Criticality Disruption

**Existing literature**: EXTENSIVELY CONFIRMED.
- PCI threshold 0.31: perfectly discriminates conscious from unconscious states across propofol, midazolam, xenon (all unconscious, PCI < 0.31) and wakefulness, dreaming, ketamine anesthesia (all conscious, PCI > 0.31). Casali et al. (2013).
- Propofol, xenon → subcritical dynamics; ketamine → near-critical dynamics + preserved consciousness. Multiple studies 2023-2024.
- Hengen & Shew (2025), Neuron: meta-analysis of 140 datasets, criticality as homeostatic setpoint. "Mechanistically diverse causes of diminished brain functionality should be linked by compromised criticality."
- ConCrit framework (Algom & Shriki, 2026): criticality as unifying mechanistic framework for consciousness theories.

**Problem**: This prediction is no longer novel. It was novel when Gruber (2015) derived it from theoretical first principles, but the empirical consolidation of 2023-2026 has independently confirmed it. The prediction is now shared with ConCrit, the Hengen/Shew framework, and the general criticality literature.

**Distinguishing power**: ZERO — shared with multiple frameworks.

**Verdict**: DROP as a prediction. Retain as supporting evidence for FMT's criticality requirement, noting historical priority (2015 vs. 2025-2026 empirical consolidation). This strengthens the paper's credibility even though it's no longer a novel prediction.

### Prediction 6: Split-Brain Holographic Degradation

**Existing literature**:
- Pinto et al. (2017): "Split brain does not lead to split consciousness" — unified consciousness with split perception. This PARTIALLY supports FMT's prediction.
- Pinto's "conscious unity, split perception" model: single conscious agent, two unintegrated perceptual streams. Compatible with holographic degradation but explained differently (unified attention/consciousness mechanisms, not holographic storage).
- Callosotomy research generally: bilateral capacity is acknowledged, though lateralization effects are real.

**Problem**: The observable prediction — "each hemisphere retains degraded but functionally complete cognitive capacity" — is already the empirical consensus post-Pinto. The holographic MECHANISM is unique to FMT, but the observable OUTCOME is not surprising to any neuroscientist familiar with post-2017 split-brain literature.

**Distinguishing power**: Low for the observable outcome. The mechanism (holographic storage) would be distinguishing if it could be tested independently, but holographic storage per se is extremely difficult to test.

**Verdict**: DROP. The prediction's observable component is already established. The mechanism is interesting but not testable with current methods.

### Prediction 8: Sleep Architecture Reflects Criticality Maintenance

**Existing literature — sub-prediction by sub-prediction**:

**(a) Waking criticality decline**: CONFIRMED.
- Bhatt et al. (2024, Nature Neuroscience): "normal waking experience progressively disrupts criticality." Continuous 10-14 day recordings in rats. Criticality markers decline across waking.
- Meisel et al. (2013, J Neuroscience): "Fading signatures of critical brain dynamics during sustained wakefulness in humans" — power-law exponents, synchronization, cascade sizes all change as function of time awake.
- The spectral exponent α = -2 demarcates wakefulness from sleep states.

**(b) Sleep onset as radical transition**: STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
- Imperial College London (2025): "Falling asleep follows a brain tipping point" — bifurcation-style tipping point ~4.5 minutes before conventional sleep onset. Critical slowing (rise in variance and autocorrelation) precedes the tipping point.
- However: some neural markers show gradual changes alongside the abrupt transition. Stimulus-induced silent periods increase within seconds at sleep onset (abrupt), while population synchrony builds over tens of seconds (gradual). The picture is bifurcation PLUS some gradual components.

**(c) NREM/REM cycling tracks criticality**: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED.
- Spectral slope and Lempel-Ziv complexity: "Both broadband slope and complexity significantly decrease from shallow (N1) to deep NREM sleep (N3), and for REM sleep, both markers increase again." (eNeuro 2024)
- Criticality of neuronal avalanches in human sleep shows differences between NREM and REM (ScienceDirect 2023).
- Caffeine increases brain complexity and flattens 1/f slope during NREM sleep (2025), shifting brain toward critical regime — consistent with the theory.

**(d) Lucid dreaming as ESM threshold crossing**: PARTIALLY SUPPORTED.
- May 2025 (J Neuroscience): Largest harmonized EEG dataset of lucid dreaming. Source-level analyses showed significant beta power reductions in right central/parietal areas including TPJ. Gamma1/gamma2 differences at lucid dream onset (eye-signaling).
- However: the study found that "sensor-level differences between lucid dreaming and nonlucid REM sleep were minimal" — the step-like discontinuity is NOT clearly confirmed at sensor level, though source-level changes at onset are detectable.
- No study has specifically measured criticality markers at lucid dream onset.

**(e) Sleep deprivation → subcriticality**: CONFIRMED.
- Bhatt et al. (2024): deviation from criticality predicts future sleep/wake behavior better than SWA.
- Meisel et al. (2013): criticality signatures fade during sustained wakefulness.
- EEG complexity changes correlate with reaction time decline during sleep deprivation (2024-2025).

**Problem**: The core claim — that sleep restores criticality — is now CONFIRMED by Bhatt et al. (2024). The main prediction is no longer novel. However, several sub-predictions remain untested or only partially tested.

**Distinguishing power**: MODERATE for the untested sub-predictions. The overall framework ("sleep restores criticality") is shared with Hengen/Shew. BUT: the specific claim that NREM/REM cycling reflects oscillation around the critical point, and that lucid dreaming onset corresponds to ESM activation at criticality threshold, are FMT-specific.

**Verdict**: KEEP, but radically sharpen. Drop the confirmed sub-predictions (a, e) as supporting evidence rather than novel claims. Sharpen the remaining sub-predictions (b, c, d) into a single focused prediction about the NREM/REM criticality oscillation and lucid dream onset.

### Prediction 9: DID Alters Have Distinct Neural Process Signatures

**Existing literature**:
- Reinders et al. (2003, 2008, 2012, 2014): alter-specific brain activation confirmed in multiple studies. EP vs. ANP show different perfusion patterns (thalamus, dmPFC, somatosensory cortex). These patterns cannot be mimicked by healthy controls or actors.
- Schlumpf et al. (2020): DMN and salience network connectivity differs between identity states.
- 2024 BJPsych Open: combined fMRI/EEG analysis captures brain changes during identity switching.
- Systematic review (2022): functional neuroimaging consistently shows alter-dependent activation differences.

**Problem**: The BASIC prediction — "different alters show different brain activation" — is ALREADY CONFIRMED by Reinders et al. and subsequent work. However, FMT predicts something more specific: that the differences should be localized to ESM-related networks (DMN, mPFC, PCC) because each alter is a distinct ESM configuration.

**What's untested**: Whether the alter-specific patterns are specifically concentrated in self-model networks (DMN, mPFC, PCC) rather than being diffusely distributed. Existing studies show differences in multiple regions (thalamus, somatosensory, prefrontal, amygdala) without testing the specific ESM-network hypothesis.

**Distinguishing power**: MODERATE-HIGH if sharpened. No other theory predicts that DID alter differences should be specifically localized to self-model (ESM) networks. IIT would predict differences in posterior cortex integration. GNW would predict differences in global workspace ignition patterns. PP would predict differences in self-prior precision.

**Verdict**: KEEP, but sharpen to focus on the ESM-network localization claim that goes beyond existing findings.

## Andrillon's REM Sleep Objection — Assessment

Andrillon's objection: "sensory input is not entirely absent during REM sleep."

**The science**: He is factually correct. Sensory input during REM (and all sleep stages) is ATTENUATED, not absent.
- Coenen (2024, J Sleep Research): "Reaction to and processing of external stimuli is attenuated in sleep — importantly, this indicates attenuation rather than complete absence."
- Thalamic gating reduces sensory flow to approximately one-third during NREM. During REM, cholinergic input from the pons increases thalamocortical relay neuron responsiveness — meaning REM may actually have HIGHER sensory throughput than deep NREM.
- The reticular thalamic nucleus modulates (not blocks) sensory flow.

**Impact on Prediction 8**: The correction from "no sensory input" to "attenuated sensory input" is straightforward and does NOT invalidate the prediction. The prediction's core claim is about criticality oscillation, not about sensory gating. The correction should be made in the paper (it's a factual error), but it doesn't require restructuring the prediction.

**Deeper issue**: Andrillon's REM objection was likely a symptom, not the root cause of rejection. The root cause was "predictions too general." Fixing the factual error is necessary but insufficient — the predictions need substantive sharpening.

## Proposed 4 Sharpened Predictions

### Prediction A: Ego Dissolution Content Tracks Dominant Sensory Input (formerly P3)

**Sharpened statement**: During psilocybin-induced ego dissolution (≥25mg, Ego Dissolution Inventory score ≥60%), the phenomenological content of the altered identity experience — specifically, what the subject reports "being" or "becoming" — will systematically correspond to the dominant sensory modality in the controlled environment. In a within-subjects design with three conditions (visual-dominant: immersive nature scene; auditory-dominant: spatial music composition; tactile-dominant: full-body vibrational stimulation), the reported identity content will show ≥70% modality-congruent classification by blinded raters.

**Experimental design**:
- **Participants**: N=60 healthy volunteers, experienced with psychedelics (to reduce anxiety confounds), randomized to condition order (Latin square)
- **Drug**: Psilocybin 25mg oral, three sessions per participant separated by ≥2 weeks
- **Environment manipulation**: Three sensory-dominant conditions presented during the ego dissolution window (typically 60-120 min post-ingestion). Each condition maximizes one modality while minimizing others: (1) Visual: high-resolution immersive projection of natural scenes, minimal sound, still body; (2) Auditory: spatial audio composition via headphones, eyes covered, still body; (3) Tactile: full-body vibrotactile suit, eyes covered, white noise masking
- **Measurement**: (1) Real-time verbal reports during ego dissolution (audio recorded); (2) Post-session structured interview (Ego Dissolution Inventory + custom identity-content questionnaire); (3) Blinded rater classification of identity content as visual-congruent, auditory-congruent, tactile-congruent, or non-congruent
- **Primary outcome**: Classification accuracy of identity content by sensory condition. Chance = 33%. FMT predicts ≥70%.
- **Secondary outcomes**: fMRI BOLD during ego dissolution — FMT predicts the dominant sensory cortex (visual/auditory/somatosensory) will show increased coupling with DMN (ESM-related) regions during the corresponding condition
- **Control**: A fourth condition with balanced multisensory input should produce more varied, less classifiable identity content

**Quantitative markers**:
- Identity-content classification accuracy: ≥70% (chance = 33%, p < .001 with N=60)
- Sensory cortex–DMN coupling increase: Cohen's d ≥ 0.5 for dominant vs. non-dominant modality
- EDI score threshold for inclusion: ≥60% (ensures genuine ego dissolution occurred)

**What would falsify it**: Classification accuracy at or near chance (33%) across conditions. If subjects report identity content unrelated to sensory environment during genuine ego dissolution, FMT's redirectable ESM mechanism is wrong.

**What competing theories predict**:
- IIT: no prediction about identity content — only about integrated information levels
- GNW: no prediction — global workspace ignition doesn't specify identity content
- PP/REBUS: predicts self-model relaxation but does not specify what replaces it. MIGHT predict some environmental influence but not the systematic, dominant-modality-tracking pattern
- HOT: no prediction
- AST: no prediction

### Prediction B: Sub-Psychedelic Psilocybin Alleviates Anosognosia (formerly P4)

**Sharpened statement**: Administration of psilocybin at sub-ego-dissolution doses (5-10mg) to patients with anosognosia for hemiplegia (AHP) following right-hemisphere stroke will produce a measurable increase in explicit awareness of motor deficits within 2-4 hours post-administration, as measured by the Bisiach Anosognosia Scale and a structured clinical interview. The improvement will be dose-dependent and will correlate with increased EEG complexity (Lempel-Ziv) over the lesioned hemisphere.

**Experimental design**:
- **Phase 1 (case series)**: N=6 patients with persistent AHP (>1 month post-stroke), open-label, single-dose psilocybin 5mg. Primary outcome: change in Bisiach scale score at 2h, 4h, 24h, 1 week post-administration.
- **Phase 2 (if Phase 1 positive)**: N=30, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover. Three doses: placebo, 5mg, 10mg psilocybin. Concurrent EEG recording. Primary outcome: Bisiach scale change. Secondary: EEG Lempel-Ziv complexity change over lesioned hemisphere; behavioral indices of implicit vs. explicit awareness.
- **Safety**: Sub-ego-dissolution doses have established safety profiles from depression/PTSD trials. Stroke patients require cardiology clearance. Exclude recent stroke (<1 month), hemorrhagic stroke, epilepsy history.
- **Mechanism test**: If the effect is mediated by global permeability increase, then (a) the improvement should be dose-dependent, (b) EEG complexity should increase globally (not just at lesion site), (c) the effect should be temporary (wearing off as drug effect subsides), and (d) the effect should generalize across anosognosia types (not just AHP — also anosognosia for neglect, if tested).

**Quantitative markers**:
- Bisiach scale: improvement of ≥1 point (4-point scale) in ≥4/6 Phase 1 patients
- EEG Lempel-Ziv complexity: increase of ≥15% over lesioned hemisphere at peak drug effect
- Dose-response: 10mg > 5mg > placebo in Phase 2 (linear contrast, p < .05)

**What would falsify it**: No change in Bisiach scale at any dose. If psilocybin increases EEG complexity but does not improve explicit awareness, the permeability model is incomplete.

**What competing theories predict**: NOTHING. No consciousness theory predicts a psychedelic→anosognosia connection. This is the purest cross-domain test of FMT's variable-permeability principle.

### Prediction C: DID Alter Switches Produce ESM-Network-Specific Reconfigurations (formerly P9, incorporating P1 elements)

**Sharpened statement**: In DID patients, controlled alter switching will produce neural reconfiguration patterns that are (a) alter-specific and reproducible across sessions, (b) concentrated in ESM-related networks (DMN: mPFC, PCC, angular gyrus, lateral temporal cortex) rather than diffusely distributed, and (c) distinguishable from the reconfiguration patterns produced by role-playing or method acting in matched controls. Specifically, the within-patient across-alter variability in DMN functional connectivity will be ≥2× greater than within-patient within-alter variability, and the DMN-to-whole-brain variability ratio will be significantly higher in DID patients than in role-playing controls.

**Experimental design**:
- **Participants**: N=20 DID patients (diagnosed per DSM-5, confirmed by SCID-D), N=20 matched controls (professional actors)
- **Paradigm**: fMRI during controlled alter switching. DID patients: therapist-guided alter switching to 3 specified alters, 2 repetitions each (6 blocks). Controls: method-acting 3 specified character roles, 2 repetitions each (6 blocks).
- **Measurement**: (1) Whole-brain functional connectivity (Pearson correlation matrices, 400-parcel Schaefer atlas); (2) DMN-specific functional connectivity (14 DMN nodes from Yeo 7-network parcellation); (3) Multivariate pattern analysis (MVPA) classification accuracy for alter/role identity from connectivity patterns
- **Primary outcome**: DMN connectivity variability ratio (across-alter / within-alter) compared between DID and controls
- **Secondary outcomes**: (1) MVPA classification accuracy for alter identity from DMN connectivity alone vs. whole-brain connectivity — FMT predicts DMN-only classification will be as good as or better than whole-brain; (2) Network specificity index: ratio of DMN reconfiguration to whole-brain reconfiguration during switching

**Quantitative markers**:
- DMN across-alter / within-alter variability ratio: ≥2.0 in DID patients, <1.5 in controls
- MVPA classification from DMN connectivity: ≥75% (chance = 33% for 3 alters)
- DMN classification ≥ whole-brain classification accuracy (testing ESM-network specificity)

**What would falsify it**: If alter-specific patterns are diffusely distributed rather than DMN-concentrated. If MVPA classification from DMN is significantly WORSE than whole-brain classification, the ESM-network localization claim is wrong.

**What competing theories predict**:
- IIT: alter differences should be in posterior cortex integration patterns (not specifically DMN)
- GNW: alter differences should be in prefrontal ignition patterns
- PP: alter differences should be in hierarchical prediction error patterns (distributed, not DMN-specific)
- None of these specifically predict DMN-concentrated, ESM-network-specific reconfiguration

### Prediction D: Lucid Dream Onset Is a Criticality Threshold Crossing (formerly P8, sub-prediction d — sharpened)

**Sharpened statement**: The transition from non-lucid to lucid dreaming during REM sleep corresponds to a step-like increase in criticality markers (power-law exponent of neuronal avalanches approaching α = -3/2; Lempel-Ziv complexity increase ≥20%), detectable within a 10-second window around the pre-agreed eye-signaling onset. This transition will show the signature of a phase transition (critical slowing beforehand, abrupt jump at onset) rather than a gradual ramp, and will be specifically localized to ESM-related cortical regions (medial prefrontal, posterior cingulate) before spreading to other regions.

**Experimental design**:
- **Participants**: N=30 experienced lucid dreamers (self-reported ≥2 lucid dreams/month, verified in lab), screened for ability to perform pre-agreed eye signals (LRLR pattern)
- **Paradigm**: Overnight polysomnography with high-density EEG (256-channel) across 3-5 nights per participant. Participants trained to signal lucid dream onset with LRLR eye movement pattern (LaBerge paradigm). Also signal dream content changes and lucidity loss.
- **Analysis window**: 60 seconds before to 30 seconds after each verified eye signal. Compute second-by-second: (1) Lempel-Ziv complexity; (2) neuronal avalanche size distribution exponent; (3) long-range temporal correlations (DFA exponent); (4) PCI-like perturbational complexity (using spontaneous cortical events as "perturbations")
- **Primary outcome**: Step-like discontinuity in criticality markers at lucid onset. Operationalized as: rate of change in complexity measures in the 5 seconds spanning the eye signal must be ≥3× the average rate of change in the preceding 30 seconds of non-lucid REM.
- **Secondary outcomes**: (1) Source-level localization — criticality increase should appear first in mPFC/PCC (ESM regions) before spreading; (2) Critical slowing signature (increased autocorrelation and variance) in the 10-30 seconds before lucid onset; (3) Reverse pattern at lucidity loss (step-down in criticality markers)

**Quantitative markers**:
- Lempel-Ziv complexity increase: ≥20% within 10-second window at onset
- Avalanche exponent: shift toward -3/2 (critical) from non-lucid REM baseline
- Rate-of-change ratio (onset vs. baseline): ≥3.0
- Source localization: mPFC/PCC lead time ≥2 seconds before whole-brain criticality increase

**What would falsify it**: Gradual ramp in criticality markers over 30+ seconds (not step-like). Criticality markers unchanged at lucid onset. Criticality increase originating in sensory cortices rather than ESM regions.

**What competing theories predict**:
- IIT: lucid dreaming should show increased Φ in posterior cortex — different spatial prediction (posterior hot zone, not mPFC/PCC specifically)
- GNW: lucid dreaming should show prefrontal ignition — overlaps with mPFC prediction but for different reasons and without criticality-threshold framing
- PP: lucid dreaming as increased precision on self-model predictions — compatible but doesn't predict step-like criticality transition
- The COMBINATION of step-like transition + ESM-network localization + criticality markers is unique to FMT

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Reframe confirmed predictions as CONVERGING EVIDENCE, not predictions
The paper currently lists predictions that are already confirmed (P5, P8a, P8e) alongside genuinely novel ones. This is strategically terrible — it invites exactly the "too general" criticism Andrillon gave. Instead:
- Move confirmed claims to a "Empirical Convergence" section: "The following observations, established independently of the Four-Model Theory, are predicted by its axioms and constitute converging evidence..."
- This turns what Andrillon saw as weakness ("predictions too general") into strength ("theory makes correct retrodictions from independent axioms").

### 2. Every surviving prediction needs a "distinguishing table"
For each of the 4 predictions, include a table showing what IIT, GNW, PP, HOT, AST, and FMT each predict for that specific experimental outcome. Make the differences concrete and visual.

### 3. Quantitative specificity is non-negotiable
Andrillon was right that the predictions were too qualitative. Every surviving prediction now has specific effect sizes, thresholds, sample sizes, and statistical tests. This is what reviewers expect.

### 4. The "redirectable ESM" is FMT's unique contribution
Predictions A (ego dissolution) and C (DID) both test the redirectable ESM mechanism. Prediction B (anosognosia) tests variable permeability. Prediction D (lucid dreaming) tests criticality-ESM interaction. Together, they form a coherent program testing FMT's two most distinctive mechanisms: the redirectable ESM and variable permeability.

### 5. Address the REM sleep issue directly
Fix the factual error ("sensory input is not entirely absent" → "sensory input is attenuated but not abolished, with thalamic gating reducing throughput to approximately one-third"). Cite Coenen (2024). This is a one-line fix that removes a legitimate objection.
