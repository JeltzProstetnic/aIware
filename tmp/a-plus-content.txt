HEADLINE:
  The Real/Virtual Split

BODY TEXT:
  The brain maintains two kinds of models: implicit models that store
  everything the brain has learned (synaptic weights, motor programs,
  body schema) and explicit models that generate conscious experience
  in real time.

  The implicit side is "lights off" — no phenomenal experience, just
  learned structure. The explicit side is "lights on" — the vivid,
  felt simulation you call reality. This architecture explains
  anesthesia (both sides shut down), dreaming (explicit models run
  without sensory input), psychedelic states (the boundary between
  implicit and explicit becomes permeable), and split-brain phenomena
  (the self model can fork).

  The hard problem dissolves because qualia are not mysterious
  additions to physical processes. They are the format of the
  simulation itself — virtual sensory qualities that exist only inside
  the explicit models, the same way color in a video game exists only
  inside the rendering engine.
