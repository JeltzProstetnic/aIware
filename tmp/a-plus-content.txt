Headline:
The Real/Virtual Split

Body:
The brain maintains two kinds of models: implicit models that store everything the brain has learned (synaptic weights, motor programs, body schema) and explicit models that generate conscious experience in real time.

The implicit side is "lights off" — no phenomenal experience, just learned structure. The explicit side is "lights on" — the vivid, felt simulation you call reality. This architecture explains anesthesia (both sides shut down), dreaming (explicit models run without sensory input), psychedelic states (the boundary between implicit and explicit becomes permeable), and split-brain phenomena (the self model can fork).

The hard problem dissolves because qualia are not mysterious additions to physical processes. They are the format of the simulation itself — virtual sensory qualities that exist only inside the explicit models, the same way color in a video game exists only inside the rendering engine.

About the Author

Body:
Matthias Gruber developed the Four-Model Theory over two decades of independent research across neuroscience, philosophy, computer science, and theoretical physics. His published work includes research on consciousness theory, computational intelligence, and cosmological applications of self-modeling systems.

Trained in bioinformatics with a background in industrial R&D and artificial intelligence, Gruber brings an engineer's precision to questions traditionally owned by philosophers. His approach bridges computational neuroscience and the mathematics of complex systems — synthesizing insights that no single discipline has been able to produce alone.

His academic work is freely available as preprints on Zenodo and PsyArXiv. This book is the accessible version of that research, written for anyone who wants to understand consciousness without wading through equations.
