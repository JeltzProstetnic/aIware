## Core Argument

Class 4 self-containment is proliferative — the architecture generates more autonomous Class 4 subsystems. This structural tendency favors the Big Rip (fragmentation into daughter automata) over heat death (convergence to Class 1) or Big Crunch (convergence to a point).

## Three Lines of Reasoning

### 1. Structural preference (computational)
- Class 4 + SOC drives systems *away* from equilibrium, generating gradients
- Self-nesting increases computational complexity, not decreases it
- Heat death (complexity → 0) and Big Crunch (all subsystems → 1) are anti-proliferative
- Big Rip (parent → many daughters) IS self-containment at cosmological scale

### 2. Heat death requires uniform singularity disappearance (physical)
If particles are computational atoms (Planck-scale singularities), heat death requires ALL singularities to somehow thermalize uniformly. How? Protons would have to decay — proton decay is unconfirmed (current lower bound: ~10^34 years). Even if protons decay, the argument requires *every* singularity at *every* scale to dissolve. In a framework where singularities are information boundaries with Bekenstein saturation, "dissolving uniformly" is hard to make physical sense of — information boundaries don't just fade away.

### 3. Big Crunch requires gap-free singularity convergence (physical)
All singularities at every scale would need to converge into a single point without gaps. But singularity boundaries exist at every scale (Planck, particle, black hole, cosmological) with Class 4 subsystems nested between them. Clean convergence requires all these nested layers to collapse simultaneously and uniformly. The self-similar nesting of Class 4 within Class 4 makes this implausible — there's always structure at smaller scales resisting compression.

## Strength Assessment

- **As a preference argument**: Strong. Big Rip is most structurally consistent with Class 4 self-containment.
- **As a necessity argument**: Weaker. Can't rule out other endgames from self-containment alone.
- **Testable**: Yes — predicts w < -1 (phantom dark energy). Current data: w ≈ -1 (DESI 2024 hints at evolution but inconclusive).

## Weaknesses

- Self-containment is a *capacity*, not necessarily a *tendency* — Game of Life doesn't produce exponentially more glider guns
- Current observations favor cosmological constant (w = -1), not phantom energy (w < -1)
- The paper currently treats all three endgames as compatible; promoting Big Rip to "preferred" is a stronger claim
