### Brief 1: The Standard Model of Consciousness (Hero)
**Target article(s):** `index.md` (home page hero image)
**Concept:** The unified architecture of consciousness — four nested models arranged across two axes (scope and mode), with the real/virtual split visible as two distinct ontological planes. This is the "Standard Model" — a single coherent system, not a collection of parts. The image should convey: "This is the periodic table of consciousness. Everything fits."
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A central structure of four nested translucent spheres or shells — two darker/denser ones at the base (IWM, ISM — the real/implicit side, "lights off") and two luminous/glowing ones above or within (EWM, ESM — the virtual/explicit side, "lights on"). The lower pair is rendered in deep blue-steel and slate, opaque, solid, like crystalline neural architecture. The upper pair radiates amber-gold light from within, translucent, ethereal, clearly generated *from* but distinct *from* the base. Fine filaments or dendritic structures connect them. The whole structure floats against a vast dark field with subtle neural-network topology — not a brain, but the *idea* of a network at the edge of order and chaos. Two axes should be subtly implied: a horizontal axis (world vs. self) and a vertical axis (implicit vs. explicit / real vs. virtual). The overall impression is of a cosmic instrument, precise and complete.
**Mood:** Authoritative, complete, quietly awe-inspiring. The feeling of looking at something that finally makes sense — a unified map where there was previously only fragments.
**Negative:** No human faces or heads. No brain cross-sections. No cartoonish bubbles. No gradient backgrounds without structure. No text or labels in the image. No flat infographic aesthetic. No neon/cyberpunk. No religious or mystical symbolism (no halos, no mandalas, no third eyes).
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (hero banner)

### Brief 2: The Real/Virtual Split
**Target article(s):** `core-architecture/real-virtual-split.md`
**Concept:** Two ontological levels of one physical system. The real side (IWM + ISM) is the substrate — physical, structural, persistent, dark. The virtual side (EWM + ESM) is the running simulation — generated, transient, luminous. The key insight: both are physical, but experience exists only at the virtual level. Like the difference between a hard drive's magnetic patterns and the running program they produce.
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A horizontally divided scene. The lower half is dense, dark, structural — think tightly packed crystalline neural lattice in deep blue-black and steel grey, synaptic junctions visible as precise geometric nodes, frozen and stable, "lights off." The upper half emerges from it: a luminous, flowing, golden-amber field of dynamic patterns — wave-like, transient, clearly *generated* by the structure below but existing at a different level of reality. The boundary between them is not a sharp line but a zone of transformation — structures from below dissolving into flowing light above. Individual threads of connection reach upward from the crystalline base into the luminous field, like data being read from storage into active computation. The lower half should feel heavy, permanent, architectural. The upper half should feel weightless, transient, alive.
**Mood:** Revelatory. The "aha" of seeing two levels where you thought there was one. Like watching a simulation boot up from its hardware.
**Negative:** No brain imagery. No yin-yang or dualist symbolism (this is explicitly NOT dualism). No split down the middle suggesting two separate things — the point is two levels of ONE thing. No people. No text.
**Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (article image)

### Brief 3: Criticality — The Edge of Chaos
**Target article(s):** `physical-foundations/criticality.md`
**Concept:** A neural network poised at the exact tipping point between rigid order and formless chaos — Wolfram's Class 4 regime. This narrow band is where universal computation becomes possible, where consciousness can exist. Too ordered = frozen crystal. Too chaotic = noise. The edge = complex, structured, alive.
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A vast neural landscape viewed from above or at an oblique angle. On the left side, the network is frozen — regular, crystalline, geometric, repetitive patterns in cold blue-white, perfectly ordered but dead. On the right side, the network has dissolved into chaos — tangled, fragmented, sparking randomly in hot red-orange, no structure, pure noise. In the narrow center band, the network is alive: complex branching patterns in amber-gold and deep purple, neither regular nor random, exhibiting the structured complexity of Class 4 cellular automata — gliders, oscillators, propagating structures. This center band should glow more intensely than either side. Neuronal avalanches cascade through it — branching cascades of activation that follow power-law distributions, some small, some spanning the whole visible field. The transition zones where order breaks into complexity and complexity breaks into chaos should be clearly visible but organic, not mechanical.
**Mood:** Precarious beauty. The feeling of standing on a knife-edge where everything interesting happens. Tension between order and chaos, resolved into something richer than either.
**Negative:** No brain anatomy. No thermometers or gauges. No literal fire or ice (metaphors should be abstract/neural, not elemental). No text. No simple gradients — the complexity must be visually evident.
**Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (article image)

### Brief 4: Hard Problem Dissolution
**Target article(s):** `hard-problem/dissolution.md`
**Concept:** The Hard Problem of consciousness is a category error — looking for phenomenal properties at the substrate level, where they categorically do not exist. The image should convey "level confusion": someone (or something) searching the wrong floor of a building for something that only exists upstairs. The traditional framing places physical processing and subjective experience on the same level and finds an unbridgeable gap; the two-level view shows the gap was between two levels, not two things.
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A vertical scene with two distinct levels. The lower level is a dense field of substrate-level machinery — neurons, synapses, molecular structures rendered in cool blue-grey, precise, mechanical, clearly physical. A beam of searching light probes downward into this substrate, looking for something. But the substrate is empty of what it seeks — no glow, no warmth, no phenomenal quality. Above, separated by a zone of transformation, the virtual level radiates with rich phenomenal qualities — amber warmth, deep purple depth, the unmistakable presence of experience. The key visual: what the search beam is looking for is plainly visible above, but the beam is pointed in the wrong direction — downward into the substrate where it cannot be found. The "gap" that traditional philosophy sees is visible as the dark space between levels, but from the image's perspective, it is obviously just the distance between two floors, not an unbridgeable chasm. A faint path or staircase of connections links the two levels, showing they are part of one system.
**Mood:** Intellectual clarity breaking through confusion. The relief of realizing the puzzle was mis-stated. Like the moment an optical illusion resolves.
**Negative:** No question marks or text. No literal buildings or architecture. No human figures. No mystical imagery. No brain cross-sections. The metaphor should be abstract, not literal.
**Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (article image)

### Brief 5: Self-Referential Closure
**Target article(s):** `core-architecture/self-referential-closure.md`
**Concept:** A system modeling itself modeling itself — the ESM modeling the very system that generates the ESM. This recursive loop has no outside; the computation IS its own observer. The inside/outside distinction collapses. This is what distinguishes conscious self-simulation from ordinary computation (like a weather model, which has an outside). Mirrors within mirrors, but not infinite regress — a closed loop.
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A self-referential visual structure — a luminous form that curves back on itself, containing within it a smaller version of itself, which in turn contains another, creating a finite recursive loop (not infinite regress — 3-4 levels). Think of it as a luminous Ouroboros, but instead of a snake, it is a neural-computational structure: glowing amber filaments forming a closed loop where the "output" feeds back as "input." The innermost level should be identical in kind to the outermost — making visible that the model and the modeled are the same process. The background is deep void — no external vantage point exists. The structure is self-illuminating: it generates its own light, needing no external observer. Faint reflections or echoes of the structure extend into the surrounding darkness, suggesting the impossibility of viewing it from outside. The recursion should feel stable and generative, not vertiginous — this is architecture, not paradox.
**Mood:** Profound, self-contained, quietly unsettling. The feeling of looking at something that looks back at you looking at it. Intellectually intimate.
**Negative:** No literal mirrors or reflections (too cliched). No Escher-style impossible architecture (too playful). No human eyes or faces. No fractal zoom (infinite regress is not the point — closure is). No text.
**Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (article image)

**Composition:** Three interconnected energy flows forming a triangular cycle, but organic and dynamic rather than geometric. Each flows into and amplifies the next. Where they meet, the interaction points flare with bright amber-gold light — the compounding effect. The cycle spirals outward slightly, suggesting growth over time — each iteration slightly larger than the last. A fourth element, Success, appears as golden sparks or cascading light thrown off at the interaction points, arcing back into the Motivation core to close the loop. The background suggests temporal depth — faint echoes of earlier, smaller iterations visible behind the current cycle, like tree rings of intellectual development. The overall shape should suggest a dynamo or flywheel — self-sustaining, accelerating.
**Mood:** Energetic, generative, hopeful. The feeling of a system that feeds itself — once started, it compounds. Potential energy becoming kinetic.
**Negative:** No gears or mechanical metaphors (this is biological/computational, not industrial). No human figures. No text or labels. No pie charts or infographic elements. No static triangles — the image must convey motion and recursion.

### Brief 8: Ego Dissolution
**Target article(s):** `phenomena/ego-dissolution.md`
**Concept:** The ESM is not abolished during ego dissolution — it is deprived of its normal self-referential input and redirects onto whatever sensory input dominates. The boundaries of self dissolve not because the self-model stops running but because it latches onto external input. Under Salvia, people literally "become" objects in the room. The DMN (Default Mode Network) collapses, self-referential processing drops, and the ESM goes looking for a new identity source.
**Style:** Dark, atmospheric, scientific-artistic. Think "Nature cover art meets concept art." Deep purples, ambers, blacks. No cartoons, no stock photos.
**Composition:** A human silhouette — but one whose boundaries are dissolving. Not disappearing — *merging*. The figure's edges are becoming indistinguishable from the surrounding environment. On one side, the figure's boundary dissolves into visual input — a rich, detailed scene of objects, patterns, colors flowing into and through the figure's edge. On the other side, auditory or tactile patterns merge with the figure's form. The interior of the figure, where the self-model should be, is not empty — it contains a searching, amber glow (the ESM still running), reaching outward toward the environmental input, latching onto it. The normal self-referential feed (represented by faint internal pathways from the figure's core) is visibly disrupted — broken, fading, disconnected. But the ESM glow persists, now reaching outward instead of inward. The background transitions from structured (near the figure) to increasingly psychedelic/complex (further out) — but not chaotically. The visual language should suggest controllability: the dissolution follows the input, it is not random. The overall scene should feel immersive and disorienting but not frightening.
**Mood:** Awe, boundary-loss, lucid disorientation. Not terror — wonder. The feeling of the self expanding beyond its container rather than being destroyed.
**Negative:** No literal mushrooms or drug imagery. No tie-dye or 1960s psychedelic cliches. No screaming faces. No horror aesthetics. No rainbows. No fractals-for-the-sake-of-fractals. No text. The human silhouette should be abstract enough to be gender-neutral and non-specific.
**Aspect ratio:** 3:2 (article image)
