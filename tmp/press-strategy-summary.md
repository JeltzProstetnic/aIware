## The Four Key Messaging Angles

### Angle 1: The Neuroscience Breakthrough
**Target:** Science journalists, neuroscience outlets
**Hook:** "Consciousness isn't mysterious—it was just mislabeled"
**Key Point:** The Hard Problem was based on a false assumption; this theory dissolves it using a computational framework.

### Angle 2: The AI Implication
**Target:** Tech/AI journalists, AI ethics outlets
**Hook:** "We finally know what conscious AI would look like"
**Key Point:** At a moment when AI companies debate machine consciousness, here's a concrete, testable framework.

### Angle 3: The Independent Researcher Story
**Target:** General interest, profile features, podcast interviews
**Hook:** "No PhD, no lab, no university—but maybe a breakthrough"
**Key Point:** Precedent for outsiders challenging established science (Mendel, Boltzmann, Wegener).

### Angle 4: The Regional Angle
**Target:** Austrian/Vorarlberg media, local news
**Hook:** "Vorarlberg researcher might have solved the consciousness puzzle"
**Key Point:** Local pride angle; outsider region producing outsider science.

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Testability is your differentiator.** Every pitch should emphasize the 9 falsifiable predictions. This isn't philosophy; it's science.

2. **The outsider angle is an asset, not a liability.** Frame it: "How do breakthrough ideas enter established fields?" Precedent matters.

3. **Timing is everything.** The AI consciousness debate is mainstream right now. Connect every pitch to current events (ChatGPT, AI ethics, machine learning).

4. **Accessibility wins.** The pop-sci book exists for a reason. Journalists want to understand it. If interviews require a PhD, you've lost the audience.

5. **Persistence pays.** Media cycles are long. First pitches often get buried. Follow up. Some outlets take months to respond.

6. **Leverage early wins.** First published article = proof of credibility. Use it in subsequent pitches ("As featured in...").
