1. AMAZON ADS (AMS) -- SPONSORED PRODUCTS & SPONSORED BRANDS
========================================================================

OVERVIEW
Amazon Advertising for KDP books runs on a cost-per-click (CPC) model.
You only pay when a reader clicks your ad. You set your own bids and
daily budgets.

THREE AD TYPES AVAILABLE TO KDP AUTHORS:

(a) Sponsored Products (START HERE)
    - Appear in shopping results and on product detail pages
    - Available to all KDP authors, recommended for beginners
    - Target by keyword or by product/category
    - Best for driving direct sales of a specific book

(b) Sponsored Brands
    - Require 3+ eligible titles under the same author name
    - Appear below top of shopping results, within results, and on
      product pages
    - Can include custom images and video ads
    - Build author brand awareness across multiple titles
    - NOT immediately usable -- you only have one title currently

(c) Lockscreen Ads
    - Full-screen ads on Kindle e-readers and Fire tablets
    - Appear on the device wake screen
    - US-only targeting
    - Kindle eBooks only (not paperback/hardcover)
    - Highly visual -- requires strong cover art

CURRENT CPC BENCHMARKS (2025-2026):
    - Average CPC across all categories: $0.81-$1.30
    - Projected 2026 average: $1.18-$1.25
    - Low-competition niche categories (science/philosophy): $0.30-$0.75
    - High-competition categories (romance, thriller): $1.50-$3.00+
    - Books specifically tend toward the lower end

RECOMMENDED STARTING BUDGET:
    - Daily: $5-$10 per campaign
    - Monthly ceiling: $150-$300
    - Starting bid: $0.21-$0.35 for niche philosophy/science keywords
    - For your niche, you can bid lower than average -- fewer
      competitors in consciousness/philosophy of mind

CAMPAIGN STRATEGY:
    Step 1: Launch an AUTOMATIC Sponsored Products campaign
        - Let Amazon's algorithm discover which search terms convert
        - Daily budget: $5
        - Run for 2-4 weeks to collect data
        - Review the Search Term Report to find converting keywords

    Step 2: Launch a MANUAL Sponsored Products campaign
        - Use converting keywords from the automatic campaign
        - Add competitor book titles as keyword targets (product
          targeting)
        - Target comparable authors: Thomas Metzinger, Daniel Dennett,
          Antonio Damasio, Anil Seth, Christof Koch, Giulio Tononi
        - Daily budget: $5-$10
        - Start with "exact match" keywords for efficiency

    Step 3: Product targeting campaign
        - Target specific competing ASINs (similar consciousness books)
        - Your ad appears on their product pages
        - Lower CPC than keyword targeting in many cases

ACOS (ADVERTISING COST OF SALE):
    ACOS = (Ad Spend / Ad Revenue) x 100
    - Break-even ACOS = your royalty percentage
    - Paperback at $17.99 (example): ~40% royalty = ~$7.20 per sale
    - Break-even ACOS = 40% (you can spend up to 40% of list price)
    - Target ACOS: 25-35% for profitability
    - Under 70% ACOS on Kindle eBooks (70% royalty rate) = profitable

KEY PRINCIPLE: At $5/day, you risk only $150/month. Even if zero books
sell, you learn which keywords and targets have search volume. Data
acquisition is the real product of early ad spend.

2. KDP SELECT / KINDLE UNLIMITED -- ASSESSMENT
========================================================================

WHAT KDP SELECT IS:
    - 90-day enrollment periods (auto-renews)
    - Your Kindle eBook becomes exclusive to Amazon (no other eBook
      retailers: Kobo, Apple Books, Google Play, etc.)
    - Readers in Kindle Unlimited (KU) can read your book for "free"
    - You earn per page read from a global fund

CURRENT KENP PAYOUT (2025-2026):
    - $0.0041-$0.0046 per page read (fluctuates monthly)
    - As of January 2026: ~$0.00445/page
    - Global fund: ~$52-54 million/month
    - A 250-page book read cover to cover: ~$1.00-$1.15 per full read

VERDICT FOR YOUR BOOK: PROBABLY NOT WORTH IT (for now)

REASONS AGAINST:
    1. Revenue per read is low: ~$1.00 per full read vs. $5-7+ royalty
       on a paperback/hardcover sale
    2. Non-fiction completion rates are 35-45% (vs. 65-75% for romance)
       -- you'd earn ~$0.40-$0.50 per KU borrow on average
    3. KU readers skew toward binge-read fiction (romance, thriller,
       sci-fi) -- not pop-sci consciousness theory
    4. Exclusivity locks you out of Kobo, Apple Books, Google Play,
       Barnes & Noble, and direct sales
    5. Your book is primarily physical (paperback + hardcover) -- KDP
       Select only restricts the Kindle eBook
    6. Philosophy/science niche has a small but dedicated audience that
       buys -- they don't subscribe to KU for pop-sci

WHEN KDP SELECT COULD MAKE SENSE:
    - If you publish a Kindle edition AND want to use Countdown Deals
      or Free Book Promotions (both require KDP Select enrollment)
    - If you have zero traction and want to try a visibility boost via
      KU's recommendation algorithm
    - If you're willing to sacrifice per-sale revenue for exposure

RECOMMENDATION:
    Skip KDP Select initially. Focus on building direct sales through
    Amazon Ads, reviews, and organic discoverability. Revisit after
    6 months if sales remain flat. If you do enroll, it's only the
    eBook that's locked -- print editions are unaffected.

3. KDP PROMOTIONAL TOOLS
========================================================================

(a) KINDLE COUNTDOWN DEALS (requires KDP Select)
    - Temporarily discount your Kindle eBook while keeping 70% royalty
    - Minimum book price: $2.99 regular price
    - Duration: 1 hour to 7 days
    - Up to 5 price increments (tiered countdown)
    - Eligibility: book enrolled in KDP Select for 30+ days
    - Available on Amazon.com and Amazon.co.uk only
    - Amazon shows a countdown timer + original price (urgency cue)
    - One Countdown Deal per 90-day KDP Select period
    - Best timed with review/marketing push for compounding effect

(b) FREE BOOK PROMOTIONS (requires KDP Select)
    - Make your Kindle eBook free for up to 5 days per 90-day period
    - No royalties during free period
    - Drives downloads, reviews, visibility, and "also bought" data
    - Cannot run in same 90-day period as a Countdown Deal
    - Free books appear in separate "Top Free" charts (high visibility)
    - Strategy: run free promo, collect reviews, then switch to
      paid with reviews as social proof

(c) PRE-ORDER STRATEGY
    - Available for Kindle eBooks (up to 1 year before release)
    - Pre-orders count toward release-week sales rank
    - Good for building anticipation through email/social
    - NOT applicable to your situation (book is already published)
    - Could be used if you publish a revised "2nd edition"

NOTE: Countdown Deals and Free Promos both require KDP Select
enrollment (see Section 2). If you skip KDP Select, these tools are
unavailable. This is a genuine trade-off.

4. A+ CONTENT (FORMERLY ENHANCED BRAND CONTENT)
========================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
    A+ Content lets you add rich media (images, text, comparison tables,
    custom layouts) to the "From the Publisher" section of your Amazon
    product page. It replaces the default plain-text description area
    with a visually engaging layout.

AVAILABILITY: Yes, available to all KDP-published books. Free to use.

SETUP PROCESS:
    1. Log in to KDP dashboard
    2. Go to Marketing tab
    3. Select A+ Content Manager
    4. Choose your marketplace (US, UK, DE, etc.)
    5. Select from 17 layout modules
    6. Design your content with images + text
    7. Apply to one or more book ASINs
    8. Submit for Amazon review (typically approved within 7 days)

MODULE TYPES (select the most relevant):
    - Comparison chart (compare your book to concept frameworks)
    - Image with text overlay (book cover, author photo, diagrams)
    - Standard image and text (explain the theory visually)
    - Multiple images with text (chapter highlights, key arguments)
    - Text-only (extended description, endorsements)

WHAT TO INCLUDE FOR YOUR BOOK:
    1. Author credibility section: your background in consciousness
       research, Ivoclar R&D role, academic publications
    2. The bubble diagram (figures/figure2-real-virtual-split-simple.png)
       -- this IS the visual hook for FMT
    3. "What you'll learn" section: key insights, chapter overview
    4. Comparison with popular frameworks: How FMT relates to IIT,
       Global Workspace, Higher-Order Theories
    5. Endorsement quotes (if/when available)

IMPACT: Amazon's own study (100,000 ASINs, 90 days) found A+ Content
produced an average 5.6% sales increase. For a niche book, the
conversion improvement could be higher since your audience is
research-oriented and responds to depth.

CRITICAL RULES:
    - No time-sensitive claims ("limited time offer")
    - No customer review quotes (those go in Editorial Reviews)
    - No external links or contact information
    - No competitor disparagement
    - Images: minimum 970px wide, white or transparent background
    - Text must be original (not copy-pasted from the book description)

5. AMAZON AUTHOR CENTRAL
========================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
    Author Central is your public author profile on Amazon. It's
    separate from your KDP publishing account. Your Author Page
    appears when readers click your name on any of your book listings.

URL: https://author.amazon.com

SETUP CHECKLIST:

    [ ] Create/claim your Author Central account
    [ ] Link all book editions (paperback, hardcover, Kindle if exists)
    [ ] Write a compelling author biography (see below)
    [ ] Upload a professional author photo
    [ ] Add your blog feed or website URL
    [ ] Add Editorial Reviews (see below)
    [ ] Set up Author Page on each relevant Amazon marketplace
        (US, UK, DE, FR, IT, ES -- important for a German-language
        author with English-language book)

AUTHOR BIO OPTIMIZATION:
    Your bio is the most important text on your Author Page. It's a
    sales pitch. Structure for your case:

    Paragraph 1: Hook. What makes you credible on consciousness?
        "Matthias Gruber is an R&D AI Transformation Manager who has
        spent [X] years investigating the computational foundations
        of consciousness..."

    Paragraph 2: Academic credibility.
        Published research on consciousness theory (Four-Model Theory),
        intelligence theory (RIM), and cosmological applications.
        Preprints on Zenodo, PsyArXiv. Conference submissions to
        ASSC, CogSci, MoMa, AICE.

    Paragraph 3: Why this book matters.
        Bridge between German-language theoretical tradition (Metzinger,
        von Neumann) and accessible pop-science. "The Simulation You
        Call 'I'" presents a unified theory of consciousness that
        dissolves the hard problem.

    Paragraph 4: Personal element.
        Vorarlberg, Austria. Industrial R&D background. Influenced by
        uncle Bruno J. Gruber (theoretical physics).

EDITORIAL REVIEWS:
    These appear prominently at the TOP of your book's product page.
    They are professional endorsements -- not customer reviews.

    How to add:
        Author Central > Books tab > Select your book > Edit >
        Editorial Reviews section > Add review

    Legitimate sources for editorial reviews:
        - Expert endorsements from academics (if any researchers reply
          positively to your outreach -- Mediano, Nilsen, etc.)
        - Professional book review services (Kirkus Indie, Foreword
          Clarion, BlueInk, IndieReader)
        - Blog reviewers who specialize in consciousness/philosophy
        - Conference presentation feedback (if published)
        - Quotes from published peer review responses

    Paid review services (for Editorial Review section):
        - Kirkus Indie: ~$425-$575 (reputable, well-known)
        - Foreword Clarion: ~$499 (appears in Foreword Reviews)
        - BlueInk Review: ~$395-$495 (for self-published books)
        - IndieReader: ~$225 (includes marketing if IR Approved)
        - Self-Publishing Review: ~$100-$250

    These are legitimate. They provide honest reviews (may be negative).
    But the brand names carry weight in Editorial Reviews section.

6. CATEGORY & KEYWORD OPTIMIZATION
========================================================================

CATEGORIES:

KDP allows 3 categories during initial setup. You can email KDP
support (kdp-support@amazon.com) to request up to 10 total.

RECOMMENDED PRIMARY CATEGORIES (select 3 in KDP):
    1. Science > Philosophy & Social Aspects of Science
    2. Philosophy > Mind & Body > Philosophy of the Mind
    3. Computers & Technology > Computer Science > Artificial
       Intelligence > Philosophy

ADDITIONAL CATEGORIES TO REQUEST VIA EMAIL (up to 7 more):
    4. Science > Cognitive Science
    5. Philosophy > Consciousness & Thought
    6. Psychology > Cognitive Psychology & Cognition
    7. Medical Books > Neuroscience
    8. Philosophy > Metaphysics
    9. Science > Physics > Quantum Theory
    10. Computers & Technology > Artificial Intelligence > Theory

STRATEGY: Target categories where your book can realistically rank in
the top 10-20. A #1 Best Seller badge in "Philosophy > Consciousness &
Thought" is achievable with even 5-10 sales in a single day. Ranking
in a tiny category is better than being invisible in a large one.

To check competition, search each category on Amazon and look at the
Best Seller Rank (BSR) of the current #1. If #1 has BSR >100,000, the
category is achievable.

KEYWORDS (7 keyword slots in KDP):

Each slot allows up to 50 characters. Use phrases, not single words.
Amazon uses these for search matching.

RECOMMENDED 7 KEYWORD PHRASES:
    1. consciousness theory philosophy of mind
    2. hard problem of consciousness qualia
    3. self model brain simulation
    4. artificial consciousness computational mind
    5. neuroscience of consciousness free will
    6. Metzinger Dennett consciousness theory
    7. mind body problem science pop science

WHY THESE WORK:
    - Combine core topic terms readers actually search for
    - Include competitor author names (Metzinger, Dennett) -- legal and
      standard practice for keyword targeting
    - Mix academic terms (qualia, hard problem) with accessible terms
      (brain simulation, pop science)
    - Cover the AI consciousness angle for tech-curious readers
    - Long enough to capture long-tail searches but focused enough to
      stay relevant

IMPORTANT: Keywords don't need to repeat what's already in the title
or subtitle. Amazon indexes title, subtitle, AND keywords together.

7. REVIEW ACQUISITION STRATEGIES
========================================================================

LEGITIMATE REVIEW STRATEGIES (Amazon-compliant):

(a) ADVANCE REVIEW COPIES (ARCs)
    Even though your book is already published, you can still distribute
    ARCs. The concept applies to any book seeking initial reviews.

    Platforms:

    BookSirens (RECOMMENDED FIRST)
        - Promote plan: $10/book + $2/reader download
        - Author plan: $100/year (unlimited books, no per-reader fee)
        - ~75% of downloaders post a review
        - Affordable entry point for niche nonfiction
        - Caveat: nonfiction results are "hit or miss" per their own
          documentation

    BookSprout
        - Growing Author: $90/year (1 campaign, 25 reviewers max)
        - Pro Author: $190/year (3 campaigns, 50 reviewers max)
        - Good for structured ARC campaigns

    NetGalley
        - 6-month listing: $450-$849
        - IBPA members: $179-$499
        - Large reviewer pool but expensive
        - Better suited to traditionally published or well-funded indie
        - Probably overkill for your current stage

    Reedsy Discovery
        - $50 one-time listing fee
        - Book must be recently published (within 6 months) or upcoming
        - Mixed reviews from authors about actual value
        - Cheap enough to try

    Hidden Gems
        - ARC distribution to genre-matched readers
        - Focus on fiction but accepts nonfiction
        - Pricing varies

(b) DIRECT OUTREACH FOR REVIEWS
    - Email colleagues, friends, academic contacts who would genuinely
      read the book
    - Ask researchers who responded positively to your outreach
      (Mediano, Nilsen) if they'd review on Amazon/Goodreads
    - Post in relevant Reddit communities (r/consciousness,
      r/philosophy, r/neuroscience) -- not "buy my book" but genuine
      discussion inviting feedback
    - Consciousness-related Facebook groups, Discord servers

(c) GOODREADS
    - Create an author profile on Goodreads (owned by Amazon)
    - Add your book, write a description
    - Join and participate in relevant groups:
        - "Philosophy" group
        - "Science and Inquiry" group
        - "Goodreads Authors/Readers" group
    - Goodreads Giveaways: physical book giveaways drive "want to read"
      shelving (even from non-winners) and reviews from winners
    - Giveaway cost: ~$119 listing fee + book/shipping costs
    - Giveaways increase Goodreads visibility significantly

(d) PROFESSIONAL REVIEW SERVICES (for both reviews AND editorial
    reviews on Amazon)
    - Kirkus Indie: $425-$575, well-recognized brand
    - Foreword Clarion: $499, listed in Foreword Reviews magazine
    - BlueInk: $395-$495, respected for self-published titles
    - IndieReader: ~$225, includes marketing push if "IR Approved"
    - Independent Book Review: varies, niche indie focus

    These provide honest (possibly negative) professional reviews.
    Positive ones can be added to your Amazon Editorial Reviews section.

WHAT NOT TO DO:
    - Never pay for Amazon customer reviews (against TOS, can get
      your account suspended)
    - Never exchange reviews with other authors (review swaps are
      detectable and against TOS)
    - Never ask family members to review (Amazon flags same-household
      accounts)
    - Never offer incentives for reviews (free products, discounts)

8. RELAUNCH STRATEGY FOR ZERO-SALES BOOKS
========================================================================

Yes, you CAN relaunch a book that's already listed with zero sales.
There are two approaches:

APPROACH A: SOFT RELAUNCH (metadata refresh -- RECOMMENDED)
    No new ISBN, no new listing. Update everything you can:

    1. Rewrite the book description (blurb)
       - This is the single highest-impact change
       - Use the problem-agitation-solution formula:
         * Problem: "Why does consciousness feel like anything?"
         * Agitation: "Existing theories explain the correlates but not
           the experience itself."
         * Solution: "This book presents a computational framework
           that dissolves the hard problem entirely."
       - Authors report conversion rate improvements from <1% to 4%+
         just from rewriting the blurb

    2. Update the cover (if needed)
       - Your cover is already designed and KDP-published
       - Evaluate honestly: does it compete on thumbnail with
         comparable books? (Anil Seth's "Being You," Philip Goff's
         "Galileo's Error," etc.)

    3. Optimize keywords (see Section 6)

    4. Request additional categories (see Section 6)

    5. Add A+ Content (see Section 4)

    6. Set up Author Central (see Section 5)

    7. Start Amazon Ads campaign (see Section 1)

    8. Launch ARC campaign for reviews (see Section 7)

    Do steps 1-6 BEFORE starting ads (step 7). Ads drive traffic to
    your product page -- if the page doesn't convert, ad spend is
    wasted.

APPROACH B: HARD RELAUNCH (new edition)
    New ISBN, new listing, treated as a new product.

    When to consider:
    - Major manuscript revision (>10% content change)
    - Title or subtitle change
    - You want a clean sales rank start
    - Your current listing has negative reviews you want to leave behind

    Drawbacks:
    - New ASIN means new product page, zero reviews, zero rank
    - Existing reviews do NOT transfer automatically (you must email
      Amazon support to request review migration, and they may refuse)
    - Any existing links/citations to the old listing break
    - Requires a new ISBN (you already have US paperback and hardcover
      ISBNs -- a new edition needs new ones)

    RECOMMENDATION: Use Approach A first. Only hard relaunch if you
    make substantial content revisions worth marketing as a "2nd
    edition."

RELAUNCH TIMELINE (SOFT RELAUNCH):
    Week 1-2: Update metadata, blurb, keywords, categories, A+
              Content, Author Central
    Week 3-4: Distribute ARCs (BookSirens, direct outreach)
    Week 5-6: Start Amazon Ads (automatic campaign first)
    Week 7-8: Optimize ads based on data, add manual campaigns
    Week 9-12: Review acquisition push, Goodreads activity, consider
               editorial reviews
    Ongoing: Maintain $5-10/day ad spend, monitor ACOS, iterate

========================================================================
KEY TAKEAWAYS
========================================================================

1. Your biggest lever is AMAZON ADS at $5-$10/day. In your niche,
   CPCs are low ($0.30-$0.75) and competition is thin. Even modest
   spend generates data and visibility.

2. Skip KDP Select for now. The KU audience doesn't match your
   reader profile, and exclusivity costs you distribution channels.

3. A+ Content is free and underused. The bubble diagram alone makes
   your listing more compelling than 90% of consciousness books.

4. Reviews are the bottleneck. With zero reviews, conversion rates
   are terrible. BookSirens ($60) is the cheapest path to 10-15
   reviews.

5. Category strategy can get you a "Best Seller" badge with minimal
   sales. In niche subcategories, 5-10 sales in one day can put you
   at #1.

6. A soft relaunch (metadata refresh) is the right move. Don't
   unpublish or create a new edition unless you've made substantial
   content changes.

7. Your academic outreach IS your marketing. Every researcher who
   reads the book is a potential Amazon reviewer, citation source,
   and word-of-mouth amplifier.
